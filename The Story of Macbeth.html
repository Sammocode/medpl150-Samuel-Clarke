The Story of Macbeth

By William Shakespeare

Illustrated by Samuel Clarke

Act 1, Scene 3- The 3 Witches Prophecies

First Witch

All hail, Macbeth! Hail to thee, thane of Glamis!

Second Witch

All hail, Macbeth! Hail to thee, thane of Cawdor!

Third Witch

All hail, Macbeth, that shalt be king hereafter!

First Witch

Lesser than Macbeth and greater.

Second Witch

Not so happy, yet much happier.

Third Witch

Thou shalt get kings, though thou be none. So all hail, Macbeth and Banquo!

In this scene, the three witches greet Macbeth with three titles: Thane of Glamis, Thane of Cawdor, and future King of Scotland; foretelling his rise to power. They also speak to Banquo, offering paradoxical prophecies: he will be “lesser than Macbeth and greater,” “not so happy, yet much happier,” and though he won’t be king himself, his descendants will be kings. These cryptic messages spark ambition and foreshadow the tension between fate, power, and legacy.
