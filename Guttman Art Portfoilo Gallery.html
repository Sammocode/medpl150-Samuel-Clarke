Photography Changes Everything / Relationship with Photography

Photography isn’t just for social media—it’s memory, proof, and emotional mapping. I take pictures of food, places, and moments to remember what mattered.

In high school, I documented art walls—mine and others’—because they inspired future works. My phone isn’t perfect, but I edit to make meaning visible.

YouTube tech videos compare phones to cameras, but I use mine to reference, reflect, and remember. My parents used photos to preserve the past, and that shaped me.

The first collage was my digital debut—using BeFunky to build surreal landscapes. It’s not perfect, but it’s mine. Mario in a cabin of hell, defending ice cream from a creature. A story told through chaos and color.

The second collage is about me—my sketches, my outfits, my flaws. I’m not perfect, and I don’t need to be. These drawings are my archive. Without them, I wouldn’t be me.
